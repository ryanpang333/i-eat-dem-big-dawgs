🐾 Paw Finder — Presentation Script

Intro

Hi everyone! My project is called Paw Finder. It's a website that helps people find lost pets and reunite them with their families.

1. Who is this for?

Paw Finder is for anyone who has lost a pet, or found one wandering around.

Think about how scary it is when a dog or cat goes missing. People put up paper posters on poles, but only the people walking by that one street ever see them. Paw Finder is for those pet owners — but it's also for kind neighbours and animal lovers who spot a lost pet and want to help return it home.

So really, it's for two groups: the people who lost a pet, and the people who can help find one.

2. What does it do?

Paw Finder lets you do a few main things:

  • You can post a lost pet with a photo, its colour, size, and where you last saw it.
  • You can also post a pet you found, so the owner can spot it.
  • There's a Browse page where you can search and filter pets by animal, colour, size, and location.
  • There's a map that shows where every pet was lost or found, plus nearby animal shelters and vets.
  • People can leave tips and sightings — like "I saw this dog near the park!"
  • When a pet gets home safe, you press the "Reunited!" button, and it adds to our counter of happy endings.

It even has some extra features I'm proud of: you have to make an account to log in, you can pick your country so you only see pets near you — it works for almost every country in the world — and there's a dark mode, a print-a-poster button, and ways to share a pet on social media.

3. How do I know it works?

To make sure Paw Finder actually works, I gave it to a few of my friends to test.

I asked them to try posting a lost pet, search for pets on the browse page, and press the Reunited button. While they tested it, I watched for anything that broke or was confusing.

They found a couple of bugs — for example, the Reunited button gave an error at first — so I went back and fixed them, and then had them try again to make sure it worked. After their testing, the posting, searching, map, accounts, and Reunited button all worked correctly.

So I know Paw Finder works because real people used it, found the problems, and I fixed them.

Ending

That's Paw Finder — a website to help bring lost pets home. Thank you for listening! Are there any questions?
